Regional Carbon Element

Sovereign carbon linking under the Paris Agreement Article 6 treaty system.

Governing Laws

Paris Agreement, Article 6, Clauses 6.2 and 6.4 [3].

Key Policies & Legal Clauses

ITMOs (Internationally Transferred Mitigation Outcomes): Bilateral trading mechanism that allows countries to sell excess emission reductions.

Corresponding Adjustment: A mandatory ledger balancing rule that prevents double-counting by subtracting emissions from the buyer's tally and adding them back to the seller's.

Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) Timeline

1
Bilateral Treaty

Two sovereign nations sign an Article 6.2 agreement establishing trade protocols.

2
Project Validation

A decarbonization project in the host country is registered and verified under strict sovereign MRV.

3
ITMO Authorization

The host country authorizes the emission reductions as ITMOs and applies a Corresponding Adjustment.

4
Transfer & Credit

Credits are digitally transferred to the buyer country's registry to meet its official climate targets (NDCs).
